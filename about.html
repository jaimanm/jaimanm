My Story

My fascination with technology began early, but it was during my time at Ridge High School that I discovered my passion for computer science and quantum mechanics. What started as curiosity about how computers work evolved into a deep interest in the fundamental principles that govern our universe.

At the University of Maryland, I'm pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science with a concentration in Machine Learning, complemented by minors in Statistics and Quantum Science & Engineering. This interdisciplinary approach allows me to explore the fascinating intersections between quantum computing, artificial intelligence, and software engineering.

My research at the National Quantum Laboratory involves working with real IonQ quantum processors, where I've achieved over 99% accuracy on MNIST classification using hybrid quantum models. This work represents the cutting edge of quantum machine learning, where we're pushing the boundaries of what's possible with current quantum hardware.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about leadership and mentorship. As Technical Lead for the IonQ Team at UMD App Dev Club and Applied Track Lead for Do Quantum, I guide teams of engineers and researchers in exploring quantum algorithm applications across industries including finance, healthcare, and environmental science.
